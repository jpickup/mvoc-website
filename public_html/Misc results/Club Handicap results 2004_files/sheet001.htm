MV Handicap Champs
Target times are based on class speeds which came from Peter Burt.
These are a little less onerous than speeds used for SE League.
Only A/L classes score in the Handicap
In this competition, people get the target for their true class, so don't get penalised for running up.
The final score is actual time:target time as a percentage - lower is better.
The people without positions against their names were on short courses or ran down
The Saxons event at Hindleap will double as the Mole Valley Handicap Championships. Adult Moles running ¨L¨ courses will be scored against the South-East League target speed for their age class, but ¨S¨ courses do not count in the Handicap. The rules for juniors used to be similar, except for ¨A¨ & ¨B¨ replacing ¨L¨ & ¨S¨. With these courses having been abolished, there is now a table to determine the shortest course that each age group can run while still being eligible for the Mole Handicap:
M9- M10 M12 M14 M16 M18 M20
 JM1   JM2   JM3   JM4   JM5M   JM5L   JM5L 
W9- W10 W12 W14 W16 W18 W20
 JW1   JW2   JW3   JW4   JW5S   JW5L   JW5L 
Running up is still allowed. So a W10 could compete in the handicap on JW2, JW3, JW4 etc; in fact any course except JW1. A M14 could run JM4, JM5S, JM5M, JM5L or even M21L.
The reigning handicap champions, who were the best senior and junior respectively at Worthlodge Forest in March 2003, are Julianna Grant and Stephen Green.
