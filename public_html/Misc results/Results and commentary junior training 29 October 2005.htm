Mole Valley Orienteering Club 

Junior Coaching Day 29th October 2005

 Joint organiser’s comments

 This was the first event of this type that Mole Valley has put on. The idea was that we should try and improve the skills base of our youngsters. Every Mole youngster from 7 years to 16 years was personally invited. We are hoping that from this small acorn a regular event will grow so that our youngsters will gain the skills to thrive in orienteering in the future. To that end we planned a fun and skill packed day. We were really lucky with the weather enjoying a warm, sunny, Autumn day, even if it was a bit wet underfoot.  

Four individual Mole juniors attended the event with their parents also enjoying the challenges. We were also fortunate that St Bede’s School, Redhill, an affiliated school, sent a contingent of three juniors. We gained two extra enthusiastic juniors whose father had spotted Julianna putting out the controls and enquired if his family could take part. 

The activities included a map walk for map reading skills, a star game to improve control finding, an individual yellow/orange course, and a relay yellow course. These were tailored to the participants who ranged from beginners to yellow/orange standard. 

A big thank you from the planners, Julianna Grant and Susan Rowlett, to the following Moles without who’s help it would have been difficult to run the event:
