PLANNER'S REPORT

I have always enjoyed Ranmore, and this time was no exception as I had several splendid days in the forest during the planning process.  I only got soaked once.  I hope you enjoyed it as much as I did, but it is admittedly not the most comfortable forest to run through.  The foliage was thicker than it has a right to be in November, and the vegetation is so variable it is very difficult to map accurately. 

 I tried to give the technical courses maximum route choice, which often meant either a long way round by paths or a steep scramble up and down hill.  I hope your navigational skills were exercised not least by often being forced off course by thicker than expected vegetation.

 The junior courses and red do tend to take up more than their fair share of planning time, so it was a pity numbers on these were fairly small.  However it was good to see such competition for the veterans championship (and on the string course).

 I am sorry that one or two of you were confused by the extra tape with the same number in a small depression near the re-entrant control 110.  This was my original planned site but as I could not find it again on my next visit I decided to abandon it.  However I should have used a different number.  Tapes at all other unused sites were removed before the event.

Simon Ling                                                     

CONTROLLER'S REPORT

Simon set some challenging courses with plenty of route choice options. Only 41 controls were used for the eight courses, which just shows that you do not need large numbers of legs to create a challenging course. Indeed, any more and the route choices would have been fewer. 

Although the majority of runners enjoyed the challenge, there were a number of you who were not too happy about the runnability depicted on the map. We took the view that one could usually take a straight-ish line provided that deviations were taken to left or right, based on what thickness could be seen ahead. Control 105 was singled out by a few as being illustrative of the problem. However, there were plenty of features nearby to enable the runner to home in to that particular control. But for the vegetation screen, the map is accurate. I feel confident that the next event held at Ranmore will see a revised map. 

One or two competitors expressed some confusion, on the longer courses, where lines, connecting the control circles, crossed one another. This is, I believe, a consequence of electronic punching, allowing doing away with second master maps. Those who attended the electronic equipment conference at Dorking the previous day heard various suggestions for dealing with this problem, such as two master maps at the start.

The weather was great, including that for the previous evenings night event. This contrasts with a run of events at Ranmore many years ago where it always rained. This prompted some wag to redraw the MV logo, depicting tons of water, and adding a motto to the logo Il Pluvait. The area became known as Rainmore.

Chris Baker (DFOK).
