It was good to see so many people out on a Tuesday evening. Nonsuch is very pleasant this time of year if you can stay out of the nettles hope you all enjoyed it. There were a couple of small hitches Nesting Skylarks signs (and indeed nesting skylarks hundreds of them) appeared 3 days before the event which required some rejigging, and the 35 kites I thought Id taken along had turned into 28 by the time we put them out, so controls 2 and 3 comprised Sainsburys bags for a while .  Control 12 was disturbed for a few minutes many thanks to the gentleman (I think it was Chris Wroe) who took time out to replace it.

 Ian Ds route was 29 30 2 3 1 12 10 11 22 9 8 27 26 13 14 15 23 24 25 16 17 18 28 19 20 4 21 5 6 7, which I think was better than the one I had in mind. For us mere mortals, I reckon the best plan was to go for 26 27 15 23 24 25 and as many on the way there and back as you had energy for.

Many thanks to Mike for processing the map corrections and overprinting the courses; to Neil for helping to put out the controls; and to Ian, Nigel and Mike for helping to collect them in again.

 Tony Schofield, Planner
