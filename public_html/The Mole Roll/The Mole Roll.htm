Mole of the Year
An annual award since 1982, inspired by Margaret Stedman, to recognise outstanding achievement or endeavour in orienteering on or off the field which has brought honour to Mole Valley.
 
It may recognise:

-

   competitive achievement (national or otherwise)

-

   competitive endeavour, great and sustained improvement

-

   service to Mole Valley in mapping, organisation, planning or always being there to help when required

-

   service to orienteering at Regional, National or International level

-

   or anything else you can think of!
 
All members are asked to nominate one individual to the previous year's holder who consults with the three previous holders.  No one individual can hold the trophy more than once in any four year period.  Awarded at the Mole Dinner

Peter Burt Trophy
A trophy presented annually to the most improved orienteer in the South East League, in memory of Mole Valley member Peter Burt, who died in May 1999 after a long battle with cancer.  It rewards personal achievement on the basis of a fiendishly complicated scoring system in the tradition of Peter's long stewardship of the League.  Usually awarded at the South East Relays.

The men run on the Blue course and the women on Green.
