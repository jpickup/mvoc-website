The area
 The history of Gatton can be traced back to the Domesday Book of 1086. Strictly speaking, the whole mapped area is Gatton Park, but for orienteering purposes, we are calling the eastern parkland section "Gatton Park", and the western woodland section "Nut Wood".

Gatton Park is now a school. The centrepiece is an impressive mansion in the Palladian Style. This is surrounded by a park landscaped by the famous "Capability" Brown, and by more recent school buildings. The grounds are managed by the Gatton Trust. The intricate gardens are out-of-bounds for orienteering on this occasion, but competitors on the technical courses will be able to enjoy running across the lawns and getting lost amongst the school buildings. Further information, including some photographs, may be found on Wikipedia and on the Gatton Trust website.

Nut Wood is now owned by the National Trust. It is semi-natural deciduous woodland on the scarp slope of the North Downs. It is less steep than most parts of the North Downs scarp, but still not flat! There is unusually little undergrowth for a chalk hill, even in midsummer. All competitors (on courses above Yellow) should be able enjoy running straight through the forest.

Mole Valley are very grateful to the Royal Alexandra & Albert School, the Gatton Trust, and the National Trust for permitting this event.

Orienteering history
 Gatton Park was mapped by CROC, the defunct Croydon club, and used a couple of times for short-distance events. Mole Valley are grateful to DFOK, who inherited the CROC areas, for loaning it to us.

Nut Wood has never previously been mapped for orienteering, although it is rumoured to have been used in the early days of orienteering in conjunction with a photocopied Ordnance Survey map.

This event will use a new 1:7,500 map with 5m contours, created by Bill Alexander and Mike Elliot in 2010/2011.
What is a Middle-Distance event?
 From the British Orienteering website (Event Guideline C):
"Middle distance orienteering requires fast, accurate orienteering for a moderately long period of time. Even small mistakes will be decisive. TD5 courses should be planned to be as consistently technically difficult as possible. Very long legs should be avoided but small and medium scale route choice is encouraged. The emphasis should be on high speed running where competitors need to adjust their speed for the complexity of the terrain. There will be a higher density of controls than for an equivalent long distance race."
South-East Championship To be competitive in the South-East Championships, you must be a member of the South-East Orienteering Association, and run a suitable course for your gender/age class as listed in the table below. If you are not sure whether you are a member of the South-East Orienteering Association, find your British Orienteering membership card, and look to see whether it says "Association SEOA".

There will be medals for the first three in each class.

 The car park and assembly will be at St. Bedes School, Carlton Road, Redhill, RH1 2LQ; grid ref. TQ274517. Hard standing car park. Car parking fee - £1. Signposted from the A23 (grid ref. TQ280513) and the A242 (grid ref. TQ267514).

Starts & Finish
 Black, Brown, Blue & Green courses will use the East start in Gatton Park. Light Green, Orange & Yellow courses will use the West start in Nut Wood. All courses use the same Finish, which is adjacent to the West start.

Both starts are 1.1km from Assembly, but in opposite directions. Both require walking along the A242 (40mph speed limit) on a pavement, and eventually crossing it. The crossing point for the West start & Finish will be marshalled. There will be no clothing transfer, and no managed clothing dump, although items may be left at the Finish at the owner's risk.

Punching starts will operate between 10:30 and 12:30 with start blocks. Courses will close at 14:00.
