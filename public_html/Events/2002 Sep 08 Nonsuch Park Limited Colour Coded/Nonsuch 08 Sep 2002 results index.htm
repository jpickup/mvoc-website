NONSUCH PARK 
LIMITED COLOUR CODED EVENT

08 SEPTEMBER 2002
 
Planner's report  Organisers' report  
Straight results (no splits) 

Split times for all colour coded courses

 

Planner’s report

Then combined limitations of the ages of the map and myself acted to produce courses which avoided the nasty bits of the map in the West, otherwise the whole map would have been almost obscured by the corrections. Personally, I am not too keen on diving into stinging nettles and brambles (usually in the wrong place) to find controls, so I made it all quite easy navigation. I did, though, make one of the legs of the White course a little more testing than I should have. The leg from the fence corner (216) to the pond bed (208) was made more complicated than it appeared on the map by some additional paths. I am sorry if anyone's run was affected by this.

For the longer courses, I rather hoped to lull one or two into falling asleep on a couple of long legs, and over-running the control. Whilst I cannot be certain, I think this worked! By and large, the controls seemed to have been roughly where people expected to find them and, fortunately, not one was taken home as a souvenir by other park users. 

It was a real pleasure to plan the event in the quieter moments of the park. However, at about 8pm on the Saturday evening I heard the distant skirl of bagpipes. At 7.30 the next morning, he or she was still playing, and the practice had not increased the choice of repertiore. I suspect the previous neighbours in somewhere like Oban had invited them to come to give the Sassenachs a treat! (You have, of course, my apologies for taking a liberty if you were both the piper and a competitor!)

Chris Mcdonald
