 Planner's Report

Being only my second time of planning, I was slightly disappointed in not having enough time to attend the beginning of the night, due to work commitments.  This meant that the whole of the start of the planners jobs for the night would be shared by other club members.  Which meant we had a job-share scheme going on.

I didn’t however get out of it completely as I rolled up at 8 o’clock and was handed a map with “Controls to Collect” on it!

It was kind of spooky out there at first, as a couple of foxes glared back when I shone my torch on them.  Not at all like that quiet afternoon, a few weeks back, when I was taping the controls. My pace quickened.

My courses were planned to be fast, and I think the Navy results reflect this.  As this was the longest course I pushed South to pick up the open bit only to fall back on the wood again for a run home. A nice contrast between wood-open-wood I think.

Ochre was nice and simple but the mud must have slowed some down, and Olive a tougher but again proving my predictions wrong as some flew round.

I definitely would like to see some Night now at the big week long orienteering events, just making it easier to get to for me.  Perhaps at the Lakes 5 days this year?

 Mark Cheesman
